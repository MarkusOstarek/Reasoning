# Revenue notes: how this field is actually financed

Last reviewed: July 2026. Companion to landscape.md.

## The headline finding

Nobody in this space runs a self-sustaining product-revenue business. Every adjacent tool is financed by philanthropy, government, academia, books, or a founder's other ventures. That is not a discouraging fact — it tells you where the money actually is.

## How each player is funded

| Player | Money source |
|---|---|
| News Literacy Project / Checkology | Pure philanthropy at scale: Knight Foundation seed, a $10M individual gift, OpenAI's People First AI Fund. ~$9.3M budget, 47 staff. Checkology is free to schools *because donors pay*. |
| Cambridge games (Bad News, Go Viral, Harmony Square) | Government and institutional: UK Cabinet Office, US DHS/Global Engagement Center, academic grants. Not businesses at all. |
| Cranky Uncle | Crowdfunding (~$15k) + university backing. Shoestring. |
| ClearerThinking.org | Subsidized by Spark Wave (founder's startup foundry) + optional donations/subscriptions. The free tools are a mission expense, not a profit center. |
| Calling Bullshit | University salaries + a Penguin Random House book deal + speaking. The course is marketing for the book, roughly. |
| Gapminder / Factfulness | Nonprofit donations + a bestselling book + (historically) Hans Rosling's speaking fees. |

## What this means for Reason Check, ranked by realism

1. **Grants and philanthropy (the field's actual business model).** Media literacy is an actively funded category: Knight Foundation, Google News Initiative, OpenAI's People First AI Fund, EU media-literacy programs, Dutch/German/Nordic national funds. A tool that is open-source, politically balanced by construction, sourced, and (eventually) validated with an academic partner is precisely what these funders want to point at. The research-partnership email doubles as grant-readiness. Realistic scale: enough to fund serious development, not a salary at first.

2. **B2B: corporate training and team assessments.** Critical-thinking / decision-hygiene training is a real corporate budget line with no dominant product. The free consumer site is the funnel; the paid product is a team version — aggregate team profiles, workshop mode, custom scenario packs for a domain (pharma communications, financial compliance, journalism, public health). This is the only path on this list with genuine profit potential, and it only opens after consumer traction proves the instrument.

3. **Book / physical derivatives.** The Calling Bullshit and Factfulness path: traction → publisher. A physical card deck of scenarios (party/family game — "what would you conclude?") is a plausible product with the same content; card games are how a lot of niche educational IP actually monetizes.

4. **Schools.** Hard to *charge* (NLP gives Checkology away, donor-funded), but school distribution is itself fundable by grants — teacher mode becomes a grant deliverable, not a product.

5. **Donations.** A quiet "support this" link once traffic exists. Modest, but costless and trust-compatible.

## What to refuse

- **Ads** — an attention-economy revenue stream inside a tool about the attention economy's failure modes poisons the entire premise.
- **Paywalling the core test** — kills both mission and virality; the free test is the asset everything else is built on.
- **Selling or monetizing user data** — the "nothing stored" promise is a differentiator; keep it.

## Practical sequence

Nothing here is actionable before traction except staying grant-ready (already true: open bank, balance audit, sources, dispute mechanism) and sending the research outreach. Revisit after the first few hundred organic users: grants first, B2B pilot second, physical/book only on real momentum.
