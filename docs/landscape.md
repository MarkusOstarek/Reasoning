# Landscape: existing tools adjacent to Reason Check

Last reviewed: July 2026. Purpose: know the field, steal what works, and be able to say precisely what Reason Check does that nothing else does.

## 1. Misinformation "inoculation" games (closest in spirit)

| Tool | Maker | Format | What it teaches |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Bad News](https://www.getbadnews.com/) | Cambridge SDM Lab + DROG | Play the fake-news tycoon | Manipulation techniques: impersonation, emotional language, polarization, conspiracy, discredit, trolling |
| [Go Viral!](https://www.goviralgame.com/) | Cambridge + UK Cabinet Office | 5-min COVID-specific version | Fake experts, emotional manipulation, conspiracy framing |
| [Harmony Square](https://harmonysquare.game/) | Cambridge + US DHS/Global Engagement Center | Play a political "chaos agent" | Election disinformation techniques |
| [Cranky Uncle](https://crankyuncle.com/) | John Cook (Monash) | Cartoon quiz app, free iOS/Android | FLICC science-denial techniques: fake experts, cherry-picking, logical fallacies, impossible expectations, conspiracy |

**Evidence base:** strong — repeated RCT-style studies show reduced perceived reliability of manipulative content, effects lasting weeks-to-months with boosters.
**Key difference from Reason Check:** these teach you to recognize *rhetorical manipulation techniques*, mostly on invented content. None asks "given this true statistic, what conclusion follows?" — the manipulative *reasoning-with-true-facts* space is left open.
**Worth stealing:** their booster logic (effects decay without repetition → our daily mode), and their villain-perspective humor.

## 2. Susceptibility assessments (closest in mechanic)

| Tool | Maker | Format | What it measures |
|---|---|---|---|
| [MIST](https://yourmist.streamlit.app/) (Misinformation Susceptibility Test) | Cambridge SDM Lab | Rate 16–20 headlines real/fake | Veracity discernment, split into real-news naïvety and fake-news cynicism |
| [Cognitive Reflection Test](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_reflection_test) | Frederick 2005 | 3–7 word problems (bat-and-ball) | Tendency to override intuitive-but-wrong answers |
| [Cognitive Bias Lab quiz](https://www.cognitivebiaslab.com/quiz/) | Independent | 10 scenario MCQs | Bias knowledge across memory/decision/social/perceptual categories |

**Key difference:** MIST is the mechanical cousin (independent item ratings, credulity vs. cynicism scoring — we deliberately match this) but tests *headline classification*, not inference quality. The CRT is abstract math-flavored puzzles with no ecological packaging. Nothing here diagnoses *which* reasoning error families catch you and prescribes what to study.
**Worth stealing:** MIST's two-dimensional scoring is validated — good precedent for our credulity/cynicism split when we approach researchers.

## 3. Interactive thinking-improvement platforms

| Tool | Maker | Format | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| [ClearerThinking.org](https://www.clearerthinking.org/) | Spencer Greenberg | 75+ free interactive tools/mini-courses | Decision-making, calibration, habit formation, a political-bias test, a common-misconceptions quiz |
| [Gapminder Test](https://www.gapminder.org/test/) | Rosling family | Factfulness world-knowledge quiz | Systematic misconceptions about global development (most people score below chance) |

**Key difference:** ClearerThinking is the strongest adjacent product overall — research-based, interactive, huge library — but its unit is the *topic course/tool*, not a scenario-based diagnostic of reasoning-error susceptibility with per-family profile. Gapminder tests *what you believe*, not *how you reason*.
**Worth stealing:** ClearerThinking's practice of running original validation studies on their own instruments; Gapminder's "you scored worse than a chimpanzee" framing — humbling without being partisan.

## 4. Education / curriculum

| Resource | Maker | Format |
|---|---|---|
| [Calling Bullshit](https://www.callingbullshit.org/) | Bergstrom & West (UW) | Full open course + book: causality, statistical trickery, misleading dataviz, publication bias |
| [Checkology](https://get.checkology.org/) | News Literacy Project | Virtual classroom for schools: source evaluation, bias, algorithms |
| [Street Epistemology](https://streetepistemology.com/) | Community | Conversational technique for examining belief foundations |

**Key difference:** Calling Bullshit covers almost exactly our error families — as a *course* (hours of lectures, university framing). It's the best proof that our content territory matters, and the worst format for a casual person in a group chat. Checkology is teacher-mediated and school-bound.
**Worth stealing:** Calling Bullshit's case studies are a goldmine of scenario raw material (properly re-derived, not copied). Checkology's existence confirms the school-distribution wedge.

## The open niche, precisely stated

Every existing tool does one of: (a) teach manipulation-technique recognition on fake content, (b) classify headlines as real/fake, (c) test abstract reflection, (d) teach the material as a course, or (e) test factual beliefs.

**Nobody offers:** a short, friendly, scenario-based *diagnostic* of how you reason from **true** information — denominators, confounders, regression, selection, windows, amplification — with a per-error-family profile, a credulity-vs-cynicism read, named concepts to study, sources shown, deliberate political balance, and a daily habit loop. That's Reason Check's lane.

**The two biggest competitive risks:** (1) Cambridge or ClearerThinking could build this in a quarter if they decided to — partnering early converts the risk into an asset. (2) The lane is open partly because scenario quality is hard: every item where a domain expert would dispute the "clean" reading costs credibility the whole category depends on.
