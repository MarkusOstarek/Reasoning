A ten-minute check-up

Twelve everyday scenarios. What would you conclude?

A chart at a family dinner. A headline in your group chat. A pile of five-star testimonials. Each scenario asks the same quiet question: what does this evidence actually support? You’ll see a few possible readings of each situation — rate how much you agree with each one, then see how your reasoning holds up, one scenario at a time.

  • Not an IQ test. It looks at reasoning habits, not intelligence — and habits, unlike IQ, are very learnable.
  • Not about your politics. The scenarios draw from all directions. Careful reasoning isn’t a team.
  • Nothing stored. Everything runs in your browser. No sign-up, no tracking, and your answers vanish when you close the tab.

A note on fairness. These scenarios deliberately include claims that flatter different worldviews — natural-living ones and institution-distrusting ones alike. The reasoning patterns are the same underneath; only the packaging changes. The habits that serve you here serve you everywhere.

A snapshot, not a diagnosis. Twelve scenarios can point at your blind spots, not measure them precisely. The concepts above are all learnable in an afternoon each — and once a pattern has a name, it’s far easier to catch. Come back in a month and see.
